Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is homeopathy?
Homeopathy is a natural form of medicine that uses highly diluted substances to stimulate the body’s self-healing mechanisms. It is based on the principle of "like cures like," meaning a substance that causes symptoms in a healthy person can be used to treat similar symptoms in a sick person.
2. How does homeopathy work?
Homeopathy works by stimulating the body's natural defenses. Remedies are prepared through a process of serial dilution and succussion (vigorous shaking) which enhances their therapeutic effects despite the extreme dilution.
3. What conditions can be treated with homeopathy?
Homeopathy can address a wide range of conditions including allergies, asthma, skin conditions, digestive disorders, migraines, chronic fatigue, and emotional issues like anxiety and depression. All in all homoeopathy is suited for all health derangements at physical as well as mental plane.
4. Is homeopathy safe for children and pregnant women?
Yes, homeopathy is considered safe for both children and pregnant women as it uses highly diluted substances, reducing the risk of side effects. However, it’s always important to consult with a qualified homeopath or healthcare provider.
5. What should I expect during my first homeopathic consultation?
The first consultation typically involves a detailed discussion about your health, lifestyle, medical history, and emotional well-being. This will help Dr. Niyanta to understand your unique symptoms and choose the most appropriate remedy.
6. Are there any side effects of homeopathic treatments?
Homeopathic remedies are generally considered safe with minimal risk of side effects. However, some individuals might experience a temporary worsening of symptoms, known as a homeopathic aggravation, which usually indicates that the remedy is working.
7. How will a patient know what medicines are they taking in homoeopathy?
We aim to educate as well as treat the patient. Dr. Niyanta provides written prescriptions to all her patients. This way you will know what you are consuming and how it affects your body.
8. How long does it take for homeopathic remedies to work?
The time it takes for remedies to work can vary depending on the individual and the condition being treated. Acute conditions may respond quickly within minutes, while chronic conditions might take longer to show improvement.
9. Can I take homeopathic remedies alongside conventional medicine?
Yes, homeopathic remedies can often be taken alongside conventional medicines (if required). However, it’s important that you inform us about the ongoing treatments.
10. How should I store my homeopathic remedies?
Homeopathic remedies should be stored in a cool, dry place away from strong odors, direct sunlight, and electromagnetic fields (such as microwaves and computers).
11. What is the difference between homeopathy and herbal medicine?
Homeopathy uses highly diluted substances to stimulate the body’s healing process, while herbal medicine uses extracts from plants in their natural concentrations to treat various conditions.
12. Do homeopathic remedies interact with food or other supplements?
Generally, homeopathic remedies do not interact with food or supplements. However, it’s advised to take them before meals and avoid strong flavors like mint or coffee shortly before and after taking the remedy. Note: There is no abstinence from such substances in general during the course of medicine.

The frequency of visits depends on your individual health needs and response to treatment. Initially, you might have more frequent visits, which may become less frequent as your condition improves.

Coverage for homeopathy varies by insurance plan and region. It’s best to check with your insurance provider to understand what is covered.

Yes, homeopathy is very effective for chronic conditions as it cures by addressing the underlying imbalances in the body. It aims to improve overall health and well-being over time.
